Pasta Al Forno with Bacon Bread Crumbs

Home

Ingredients

Extra virgin olive oil, for baking dish
2 3/4 teaspoons kosher salt
1 pound short pasta, such as ziti or penne
2 bacon slices
1/4 cup dried bread crumbs
1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
1 cup heavy cream
1 1/2 cups grated aged Cheddar or Gouda cheese
1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for the top
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
4 cups baby spinach leaves
1 beefsteak or roma tomato

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven to 400.
  2. Coat a large baking dish with olive oil.
  3. Fill a large pot with water 2 inches from the top.
  4. Place over high heat and bring to a boil.
  5. Add 2 teaspoons of salt.
  6. Cook the pasta for 3 minutes less than the directions (you want it very al dente).
  7. Drain pasta in a colander and run under cold water to stop the cooking.
  8. Freeze the bacon for 10 minutes, to make it easier to chop.
  9. Thinly slice the bacon crosswise, then chop it into very small pieces.
  10. Combine the bacon, bread crumbs, and thyme in a small bowl.
  11. Return the pasta to the pot and stir in the cream, Cheddar, Parmesan, pepper, and remaining 3/4 teaspoon of salt.
  12. Fold in the spinach.
  13. Pour the pasta into the prepared baking dish.
  14. Using a serated knive, thinly slice the tomato and layer over the pasta.
  15. Sprinkle the with the bacon bread crumbs and more Parmesan.

Cooking/Baking

  • Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until the bread crumbs are golden brown and crispy.
